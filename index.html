Key Details

What is this document? Why are we collecting these data?

AICCU is required by state law to collect and submit the aggregated, unduplicated number of Associate Degree for Transfer (ADT) students admitted to the independent segment to the Governor and Legislature.

This document provides guidelines and materials for the 2020 ADT collection and will be updated with additional notes as needed. As such, if you are responsible for submitting the survey, please consider bookmarking this page.


Why are we getting this so soon?

In short, this is tied to a 10+% cut to the CalGrant that could take effect within the next month. The May Revision of Governor Newsom’s 2020-21 State Budget proposes to cut the maximum Cal Grant award for new students attending independent, nonprofit institutions by $1,028, in response to the sector not reaching 2,000 unduplicated, admitted Associate Degree for Transfer (ADT) students in the 2019-20 academic year. The way in which the academic calendar and the state budget calendar overlay, AICCU was only able to report the Fall 2019 ADT number of 1,435, before the May Revision was released.

After continued conversations with the Department of Finance and the governor’s administration, AICCU has agreed to report the number of Spring 2020 unduplicated, admitted students by July 2020. AICCU believes that with this accelerated collection, it is likely to reach 2,000 unduplicated, admitted ADT students and remove the cut to the maximum Cal Grant award. To meet this deadline, it is critical that we work together.

Reporting Process

Step Instructions
Step 1 - Review Materials Visit https://aiccu.github.io/adt and review all materials
Step 2 - Compile Records Review and complete the AICCU Template for ADT Data Submission (ADTSubmissionTemplate_SpringSummer2020.xlsx) in your assigned OneDrive folder. Instructions and example sheets are provided in the workbook, but please reach out if there are any questions.

If your institution did not have any ADT students for this collection period, please skip to Step 4.
Step 3 - Encrypt Workbook and Upload Once records are entered into the Excel file, protect the spreadsheet with a password before uploading it to the OneDrive folder. Save the password to be entered into the Exit Survey (step 5). Submit your completed Excel file to the assigned OneDrive folder no later than the end of business on Friday, June 26, 2020.
Step 4 - Complete Exit Survey Once finalized and submitted to OneDrive, please complete this Google form with your name, institution, and the password you used to create the file. This will help AICCU keep track of completed ADT submission. Please also complete this even if your institution did not have ADT students to report for the Spring/Summer 2019 period.

FAQ

We never signed an MOU with the Community Colleges - do we still need to submit data? Yes. AICCU is required by state law to collect and submit the aggregated, unduplicated number of Associate Degree for Transfer (ADT) students admitted to the independent segment to the Governor and Legislature. This is regardless of if the MOU with the Community Colleges was signed.

Should I just send ADT student records that have enrolled? No! As part of statute, we are reporting all ADT students that were admitted to AICCU institutions, not necessarily enrolled. Please send all ADT students that applied and were accepted to your institution to begin during the fall 2019 period.

Are you requesting data for all ADT students admitted to our institution or only those ADT students who are pursuing the same major with the 60-unit guarantee? All. We are requesting all records of ADT students that were admitted to your institution during this period, regardless of their expressed major at your institution. This is because the current statute requests that AICCU reports the unduplicated number of admitted ADT students in the sector.

I don’t have access to my OneDrive folder. How do I get it? You should have recently received access to the OneDrive folder, as well as a link in your initial e-mail. If you have not, please contact randy.tarnowski@aiccu.edu

The longer version: there are two criteria that must be met to qualify as an admitted/accepted ADT student: Should have been admitted/accepted to your institution to begin in the fall 2019 period: Note that this is not contingent on whether they enrolled or not. Should have identified/been identified as ADT: For the purposes of this collection, we are counting all students who identify as part of the ADT program at any point in the admission process. This could be through your institution’s application (e.g., a checkbox that allowed the student to self-identify as ADT) or through a review of the student’s transcript (did it contain an AA-T or AS-T?). We have learned that neither of these methods are perfect given that CC transcripts can be inconsistent in notating the AA-T/AS-T. This does not mean that they must be pursuing the same major as their ADT degree: We are requesting all ADT students that were admitted to your institution during this period, regardless of their expressed major at your institution. How does AICCU define an admitted student? What is the difference between admitted and accepted? AICCU considers these terms as interchangeable: that is, a student is admitted/accepted to your institution if they have been formally offered a seat at any point. We understand that terminology differs from institution to institution. If the student has been offered a space and is flagged as an ADT, please submit their record.

How do I encrypt/protect my file? Can I send it by e-mail? No. Please DO NOT send any files via e-mail. Once records are entered into the Excel file, use the “protect workbook” feature of Excel to create a password. Instructions on how to protect an Excel workbook can be found here (https://support.office.com/en-us/article/protect-an-excel-file-7359d4ae-7213-4ac2-b058-f75e9311b599). Once you upload the encrypted Excel file, enter your password in the Exit survey or e-mail it to me.

Who does AICCU want to be in charge of this process at my institution? We understand that the ADT collection process is a collaborative effort between multiple departments (Registrar, Financial Aid, Institutional Research, IT, Enrollment) and that this may differ from institution to institution. As such, we defer to your institutions on how to capture these records. We apologize for inconvenience if there are too many people included in the initial e-mails, but given turnover and bouncebacks, we have found this is often the best way given the size and scope of this important project.

Can’t AICCU pull ADT records from the community colleges? Unfortunately, not. We understand that this would be less time consuming to institutions; however, the community colleges currently do not have unified access to ADT transfers.
I have not been able to access the OneDrive folder - the landing page asks me to enter my email and an access code will be sent. I don’t receive the code. What’s the problem?
